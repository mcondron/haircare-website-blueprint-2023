Hair Tips and Tricks

Washing Your Hair

  • Occasionally masking or oiling your hair before shampooing prevents damage and locks in moisture
  • Try to wash your hair as little as possible, but the more natural oils it has, the more frequently you need to wash it
  • People with thin, oily, or straight hair often need to wash their hair more frequently, as often as every other week
  • People with thick, dry, or curly hair should avoid washing their hair more than every 3 days
  • No one should wash their hair more than once a day
  • Pay attention to where you are washing
  • Apply shampoo to your scalp
  • Apply conditioner to the middle and ends of your hair
  • Because different parts of your hair need different things, try to avoid 2-in-1 products
  • Always condition after shampooing, this allows your hair to replenish its moisture
  • Comb conditioner throug
  • h your hair with a wide-toothed comb and let it sit for a few minutes before you wash it out
  • Wash out any products you apply in the shower thoroughly to prevent build-up on your scalp
  • Use cool or lukewarm water to wash your hair
  • If you color or dye your hair, use products specially formulated for colored hair
  • Try to avoid harsh chemicals in shampoos and conditioners

Dealing With Wet Hair

  • TREAT IT GENTELY
  • Don’t aggressively brush your hair when it’s wet
  • Espcially if you have curly hair, this is a good time to detangle with a wide tooth comb
  • Don’t rub or twist your hair with a towel
  • Try patting or squeezing it dry with a cotton shirt or a microfiber towel
  • If your hair is curly or wavy, try plopping
  • Don’t sleep on wet hair
  • Air dry your hair whenever possible
  • Wait for it to be fully dry before straightening or curling your hair
  • Why? Think about what happenes to water when it’s dropped on a hot pan
  • Limit how often you blow dry your hair
  • Try to stick to no more than once a week
  • Use as low of a heat setting as you can
  • If you have curly hair, use a diffuser
  • Especially if you have curly hair, apply styling products while your hair is damp

Styling Your Hair

  • Limit how often you straighten or curl your hair
  • Excessive heat can be very damaging, so use the lowest setting possible
  • Apply a heat protection serum while your hair is still wet before applying heat
  • Try not to apply strong heat every day
  • Don’t wear tight hair styles like ponytails and buns everyday
  • Use softer hairties like scrunchies instead of plastic ones
  • Avoid using strong hold gels regularly
  • Switch up where you part your hair for more volume
  • If you have straight hair, try running a bruch through your hair while you blow dry it

Extensions and Weaves

  • Be sure to use products that are meant for dyed hair
  • Extend the time between touch ups, especially in the winter
  • Get your hair dyed at a salon whenever possible
  • If you dye your hair at home, follow all given instructions and test the color before applying it to your whole head
  • If you’re dying your hair a natural color, try to stay within three shades darker or lighter than your natural hair color
  • Limit how much bleach you use

For The Summer

  • The extra heat of summer, along with the chlorine in pools adds additional stress to your hair, so limit doing anything that damages your hair even more
  • Avoid bleaching hair as much as possible, as it is more fragile in the summer
  • Just like with your skin, avoid exposing your hair to UV rays for long periods of time
  • Lighter hair is particularly susceptible to damage
  • Wide-brimmed hats and seeking shade are great plans
  • Also try leave in conditioner with SPF
  • Don’t swim with wet hair because your hair will soak up all of the salt or chlorine in the water
  • Wet your hair with fresh water before swimming to avoid this
  • You should also wear a swim cap
  • Rinse your hair thoroughly after swimming to prevent build-ups
  • After swimming, try conditioning your hair with a special swimmer’s conditioner to replenish moisture

Sleeping

  • Again, DON’T SLEEP WITH WET HAIR
  • Invest in a silk or satin pillow case to reduce friction and breakage
  • People with curly hair may find that their hair gets especially frizzy or tangled at night, to prevent this, try sleeping with your hair in one of these protective styles
  • A loose bun
  • Braids
  • Overnight rollers

Food for Healthy Hair

  • Food with high protien
  • Food with high zinc
  • Food with high omega 3 fatty acids
  • Basicly, most healthy foods are also good for your hair
